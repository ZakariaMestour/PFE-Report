1. **Introduction**

Provided below are instructions to be respected strictly in order to ensure the required quality of the ESP report (which, unless exceptionally allowed otherwise, consists of 60 to 80 pages maximum). This text can be a template for all reports written in Word.

2. **Instructions for writing the text**

The report text as a whole uses Times New Roman for police, 12 in size, lower case, 1,5 line spacing. Paragraphs are justified, and start with a 1,25 cm indentation. A 6 pts space occurs after each paragraph. If you would like to stress the importance of a certain word, you can put it in *italics* or in **bold**, but never underline it. Avoid using capital letters in the text, except when required to respect capitalisation rules such as the first letter in a title, the beginning of a new paragraph or after certain punctuation marks (full stops ; question marks ; exclamation marks ; etc). 

1. **Inserting figures**

Figures are centred in the middle of the page. Each figure is given a title, that is centred too below the given figure. It is written as : Figure  N° – Title, **in** Times New Roman (size 12). Figure numbering is generated automatically, as exemplified in Figure 1 below. Each figure is mentioned in the explanation or discussion, before it is inserted.

2. **Inserting tables**

Tables are put inside the text. They should be put in the middle of a page, as shown in table 1 below. Each table is given a title that is placed above it. The title is written in Times New Roman (size 12\) in **bold**.

4. **General tips** 

It is advisable to adhere to the following recommendations when writing the text of your report:

* Avoid using the passive voice when introducing or describing your work « it has been done»; Use first person plural « we » instead « we have created a practical solution»;

* Use third person singular « it » as subject to present or describe your application functioning mode;

* Do not underline titles or parts of sentences.

* Do not use colours in chapter or paragraph titles.

* Avoid short paragraphs (that may consist of 1 or 2 lines).

* Avoid dots « … ». Avoid « etc. » to the maximum. Use it only when very necessary.

* The complete nouns that acronyms stand for are presented in full when acronyms are used for the first time in the text.

* Avoid empty space in your pages (caused mainly by figures). Figures should be placed in a way that limits white zones or empty space inside chapters.

* Page numbering starts with (1) on the page entitled « General introduction».

* Avoid colours unless required by charts, schemas, maps or application graphic interfaces, for example.

  5. **Reminder of punctuation rules**

No matter what punctuation mark we use (‘.’ or ‘,’ or ‘;’ or ‘:’ or ‘?’ or ‘\!’), we leave a space after it, not before. 

3. **Page formatting** 

   1. **Margins**

Page margins are organised as follows: 2,5 (top and bottom), 3 (left) and 2 (right).

2. **Headers and footers**

Page headers can contain: Chapter titles on the left (written in small capitals) in Times New Roman (size 11), with a line that separates it from the text in the page. On the other hand, page footers can contain page numbers in Times New Roman (size 10,5) centered and put in **bold**.

4. **Hierarchy of titles in sub-sections**

All section or sub-section titles are left-aligned, without indentation. Furthermore, titles and subtitles must be put on the same vertical level. The number of a paragraph title at a given level must include the numbers of the previous level. It is equally expected to adopt a uniform spacing between titles and paragraphs (in this report: 12 pts before and 6 pts after). Please avoid using colons « : » at the end of titles or subtitles.
