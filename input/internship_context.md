# Raw Context: Internship Overview & Operational Workflow

## 1. General Internship Details
*   **Company/Host Organization:** Adria
*   **Timeline:** February 2026 to July 1st, 2026 (5-month duration).
*   **Framework Followed:** Agile / Scrum methodology.
*   **Assigned Team:** Supply Chain Finance Platform Team.
*   **Assigned Academic Project:** The Disbursement Module.

---

## 2. Phase 1: Integration & Training (Weeks 1 & 2)
The first two weeks of the internship were conducted via **remote training** to establish a baseline for both business logic and the technical stack.
*   **Business Training:** Deep dive into the functional business layer of the Adria architecture.
*   **Technical Training:** Focused on mastering the core development framework: **Java** and **Spring Boot**.

---

## 3. Phase 2: Main Work & Team Operations (On-Site)
Following the two-week training period, work transitioned completely on-site. The daily responsibilities focus heavily on active participation within the core development team building the primary platform.

### Daily Software Development Lifecycle (SDLC)
*   **Task Management:** Tasks are assigned and tracked via **Jira** tickets.
*   **Version Control:** Code is pushed to **Bitbucket**.
*   **Code Review Pipeline:** Once code is pushed, the supervisor (who serves as the **Tech Lead**) reviews the Pull Request (PR). The Tech Lead either requests modifications or merges the branch.
*   **Quality Assurance:** Once merged, the ticket moves directly to the **Tester** for verification.
*   **Scrum Cadence:** Fully integrated into the team's standard Scrum ceremonies and meetings. The internship team joined during **Sprint 2** and is currently entering the beginning of **Sprint 8**.

---

## 4. Academic Project: The Disbursement Module (Parallel Work)
The assignment to build the **Disbursement Module** runs alongside daily platform tasks. 

### Strategic Execution & Timeline Friction
Because the main Supply Chain Finance platform is in its early stages (moving from Sprint 2 to Sprint 8), the core team has not yet reached the milestone sprint where the Disbursement Module will be natively injected into the architecture. 

Consequently, development on this module is treated as **parallel work**:
*   **Execution Strategy:** Development occurs dynamically during gaps in the sprint cycle or immediately upon finishing assigned Jira tickets.
*   **Functional Design Loop:** Every Monday during formal **Refinement Sessions**, direct collaboration occurs with the **Product Owner (PO)**. 
    *   The PO explains the core business concepts and requirements for the module.
    *   Functional diagrams are generated to present these concepts back to the PO.
    *   The PO evaluates the diagrams, providing validation or requesting specific modifications to align with the final architecture.
